Conversion tracking needs a clean-up before scaling
The bidding column mixes online purchases with leads, calls, store visits and local actions set as primary for goal, and every campaign runs on Maximise Clicks or Maximise Conversions - none use value-based bidding despite 13 months of purchase data. Separately, a secondary Add to Cart action reports over $349,000 of value in 30 days (it is pulling full cart value on every add), which badly inflates the all_conversions value in Google's default reports even though it is not in the bidding column. Fixing this is the prerequisite for the growth levers below.

Search term review

Negatives

The obvious move on an ecommerce account is to cut every term with zero online sales. On this account that would be a mistake. Once showroom conversions are counted, the biggest "non-converting" terms are the account's most valuable local demand, so this section is mostly about what NOT to exclude.

Do not add "pool table" as a negative
The generic "pool table" and "pool tables" searches show zero online conversions, but they run in tightly geo-targeted location campaigns and drove 159 store visits, 226 direction requests and 94 calls over the last 90 days. For high-consideration tables, someone searching near a showroom is exactly the buyer to reach. Retain these terms; the action is to value the store visits and calls (see Growth), not to exclude the searches. The one caveat is geo-targeting: confirm each campaign is limited to its store's catchment so spend is not leaking to areas with no showroom.

Performance Max

Performance Max

There is no active Performance Max campaign in this account.

Performance Max is the scale layer, not the starting point
This account already runs Standard Shopping successfully across cues, accessories and tables, so the core ecommerce surface is live and proven - unlike accounts where Shopping is missing entirely. That means Performance Max is a growth opportunity to add on top, not a gap to panic about. The right sequence is: fix conversion tracking and move Shopping to value-based bidding first, then add a single tightly-themed Performance Max campaign as a scale layer once the feed and unit economics are proven on Standard Shopping. Leading with Performance Max now, before value-based bidding and clean purchase signal, would hand spend to a black box with no clean signal to optimise against. It appears as a directional lever in the growth section, deliberately sized conservatively rather than against any industry figure.

Where to push next

Growth

The foundational lever is tracking: value the showroom actions so bidding can see them. After that, fund the proven campaigns losing visibility to budget, sized with the (budget-lost / captured) x revenue method on 90-day data. The sized budget levers total about +$2,820/mo in online revenue from existing winners; store-visit valuation and Performance Max add further upside on top.

Lever 1 - foundational
Value store visits, calls and directions in bidding
The location Tables campaigns drove 159 store visits, 226 direction requests and 94 calls in the last 90 days - the real conversions for $2,000-plus tables people want to see before buying. None of it carries a dollar value, so bidding is blind to it and these campaigns look like losers on online ROAS alone. Assign a considered value to store visits and calls, then bidding and budgets can finally reflect the showroom demand they create. This unlocks the levers below.
